The Problem is More Than Just Pollution

Plastic has more than a handful of harmful chemical properties. It's convenience in our lives has overshadowed the negative implications for many years. BPA is just one of many toxic chemicals found in plastics. However, many products now tout "BPA Free" as a safer alternative. Unfortunately, most companies simply replaced BPA with BPS, a related compound and is just as toxic. Here are just a few major health concerns associated with plastics that come in contact with food or water, in the environment and at your dinner table:

  • Elevated estrogen. In both men and women this looks like fatique,
    weight gain, breast lumps, low sex drive, infertility issues, and much more.
  • Plastic attracts toxins. Plastic in the environment acts like a homing
    beacon for summoning other toxic compounds.
  • PCBs and PAHs are just two of many toxins attracted to plastic. Both of which cause serious health problems including cancer, reproductive issues, and blood & liver abnormalities
